Michael's Gallery

Description

Imagine you had 30 seconds to learn about Michael. Gain the opportunity to take snapshots of his hand-drawn images which contain nuggets of his life and interests. Inspect the snapshots you take and try as many times as you want. You won't be able to catch everything the first time!

Initial Sketches

The following are my initial 10 sketches. They are a variety of different types of websites that attempt to highlight different aspects of Michael. In a sense, they are also a reflection, of my own journey on learning about Michael, his interests, his likes/dislikes and his overall lifestyle. In my initial meeting, I attempted to jot down the things that were important to Michael by asking about where he spends most of his time. In addition, I asked about fond memories he had and things he liked to do as a child. They vary from games to simple "discover something about Michael".

This is also important as this idea is present in 1 of Michael's final drawings.

Bop It

Description

When tasked to create a novel way of unlocking our phones, what better place to take inspiration from than childhood nostalgia. Presenting the Bop It unlock method.

Using some shakes, twists, pulls and bops, you can unlock your phone in a secure manner, with the authentic sound of the original.

In terms of its security, I would say that having a password that is at least like 6 motions long would be ideal as otherwise, it would be really easy to break in. Additionally, we do have sounds though, for highest security it would be better to turn the sound off so that others would not be able to hear it.

Accidental unlocks would pretty much be impossible with this approach as it requires a non-random complete set of movements to approve the password and fails otherwise.

Initial Sketches

The following are some initial sketches that show some of the inspiration for this idea. It was an idea that my partner Alan and I came up together and many of our sketches were quite similar as well.

Sketch 1

This is the most basic idea which pretty much everyone had which was: "track the movements of the phone and make that your password.". This idea is the base of 'Bop-It unlock method', which really constrains the movements into 4 distinct categories.

Sketch 2

This second image is quite important as it was chosen to be one of the refined sketches. It describes using the peripherals of our reach and body proportions to unlock our phones. I thought this was a good idea because it uses our unique combination of body proportions to unlock our phones, making it quite safe and easy.

Sketch 3

The idea with this was to use the microphone to sing a song which would become the password.

Sketch 4

This was to create an adapter which could enter the charging port and the adapter had a key slot for a physical key. The user would need to use the adapter and insert the correct key to unlock their phone.

Sketch 5

Many phones nowadays have moisture and water sensors present, especially in their charging ports. The user would need to carefully place their charging port into a glass of water a specific distance deep to unlock their phone.

Sketch 6

This was similar to the singing idea but adds 2 more elements. First, there is a touch component because they are playing instrument, second, their is an orientation component because the user would be able to access the piano pedals by tilting their phone is 3 specific directions.

Sketch 7

Sketch 7 plays with the idea of having a specific, limited amount of movements that are well defined to unlock our phones. It simple describes a juggle routine which would be mapped by the user on the setup of the lock.

Sketch 8

This idea was to use the same sensors that wireless chargers use and drag a magnet in a specific pattern to change the electric and magnetic field of the phone. Dragging in a specific pattern would become the password. Obviously, didn't go with this idea as it could harm the data stored in the phone (at least, I wouldn't want to test it anyways);

Sketch 9

Sketch 9 plays with the idea of having a specific, limited amount of movements that are well defined to unlock our phones. It simple describes a juggle routine which would be mapped by the user on the setup of the lock.

Sketch 10

This last sketch describes using video + machine learning to perform an exercise routine in order to unlock the phone. It would also try to capture the body proportions inherently to ensure that its the correct person.

Refined Sketches

After all of our initial sketches, we decided to refine 3 of them. Those 3 were:

  • Tilt your phone game to unlock your phone (original idea from Hima)
  • Using AR (Augmented Reality) and a map to find something notable and unique to unlock your phone (original idea from Alan)
  • Using Body Proportions to unlock your phone (my idea)

Refined Sketch 1

Using the gyroscope and accelerometer, you can tilt your phone to drain the amount of water in 6 different drains. If you get the proportions correct, you can unlock your phone.

Refined Sketch 2

Using Alan's idea for AR, I thought giving a situation of a front-door and somewhere in the augmented space, a key is hidden (in this case, underneath a rug) to unlock your phone.

Refined Sketch 3

This is a combination (no pun intended) of my idea and Alan's idea for AR where you use AR to show a lock, and like a real lock, you have to input a correct number sequence while at the same time, the phone is analyzing your hands proportions to ensure that it is you.

Initial Sketch Description

I explored some ideas that were more utility based and some others that are using proxemics to enhance our experience

I personally felt that they ideas which were the most creative were the ones that enhanced the embodied experience of something already

These can be seen in Page 2 & Page 4

Page 2

This depicts 3 sketches which show different ways that proxemics could be used to produce music. THe first sketch is like twister, but the areas are covered with detectable symbols.

The second sketch shows someone using something that has detectable symbols which are mapped to musical instruments. The position and distance from the camera cause the sound to change.

The third sketch shows a similar idea to the above except that garbage bags are lined with these symbols which can then be wrapped around other objects, making anything a musical instrument.

Page 4

This shows 2 sketches which outline different ways a play/performance could be enhanced with the use of proxemics

The first sketch outlines how characters (which would be recognized somehow) could act out a performance and a narration could occur in tandem with their performance along with music. It could use AI or something to adapt to the script. It would use the position of the characters, their distance between each other, the distance from the camera, and other parameters to contribute to the performance.

The second sketch displays a similar idea except that the performance is done using puppets instead of actors. The difference being that one person could play multiple characters as well as the fact that it is harder to tell a story with puppets than it is from a live show.

I decided to refine and storyboard my play/puppet and musical instrument ideas as I felt that proxemics could be a novel way of expressing these artistic modes
