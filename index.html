Michael's Gallery

Description

Imagine you had 30 seconds to learn about Michael. Gain the opportunity to take snapshots of his hand-drawn images which contain nuggets of his life and interests. Inspect the snapshots you take and try as many times as you want. You won't be able to catch everything the first time!

Initial Sketches

The following are my initial 10 sketches. They are a variety of different types of websites that attempt to highlight different aspects of Michael. In a sense, they are also a reflection, of my own journey on learning about Michael, his interests, his likes/dislikes and his overall lifestyle. In my initial meeting, I attempted to jot down the things that were important to Michael by asking about where he spends most of his time. In addition, I asked about fond memories he had and things he liked to do as a child. They vary from games to simple "discover something about Michael".

Sketch 1

The idea with this is that it was a discovery game where we would go on the journey to discover Michael's interests, similar to how I went a journey to discover his interests.

Initial Sketch 2

This introduces the use of a crosshair because I learned that Micheal likes photography. This is an important feature as this is something that we used in our final design. The user would be able to use the crosshair to move around and select different areas like music or books and learn about his favourites. This is also important as this idea is present in 1 of Michael's final drawings.

Initial Sketch

This is similar to the first except that there is a fog of war that the user has to traverse to learn about Michael.

Initial Sketch 4

This is exploring more of his personal day and his day-day life.

Initial Sketches 5/6

The top sketch highlights his room (which is dirty because Michael said he often procrastinates) and upon cleaning it, we learn about his favourite albums. This is idea is reflected in 2 of his handrawn images, one featuring his room, and the other featuring his favourite albums.

Initial Sketch 7

This is referencing a canvas because Michael likes to draw. This became important as Michael actually drew the backgrounds of the final assignment, highlighting the different aspects of Michael.

Initial Sketch 8

This was an idea where the user could select a piece of the background which showed nature (because Michael likes walking in nature). Clicking in specific areas would allow you to experience different things like rock climbing or walking in nature.

Initial Sketch 9/10

The top sketch is a drawing tool which would highlight Michael's interest of drawing.

The bottom sketch is a game which allowed the user to select multiple options which would increase or decrease his happy level vs energy level. This became one of the ideas which we decided to refine.

I thought our idea reflected the personality of Michael in both obvious and subtle ways which was something that I was aiming for from the beginning. It really helps that Michael can draw!

Bop It

Description

When tasked to create a novel way of unlocking our phones, what better place to take inspiration from than childhood nostalgia. Presenting the Bop It unlock method.

Using some shakes, twists, pulls and bops, you can unlock your phone in a secure manner, with the authentic sound of the original.

Initial Sketches

The following are some initial sketches that show some of the inspiration for this idea. It was an idea that my partner Alan and I came up together and many of our sketches were quite similar as well.

Sketch 1

This is the most basic idea which pretty much everyone had which was: "track the movements of the phone and make that your password.". This idea is the base of 'Bop-It unlock method', which really constrains the movements into 4 distinct categories.

Sketch 2

This second image is quite important as it was chosen to be one of the refined sketches. It describes using the peripherals of our reach and body proportions to unlock our phones. I thought this was a good idea because it uses our unique combination of body proportions to unlock our phones, making it quite safe and easy.

Sketch 3

Sketch 3 plays with the idea of having a specific, limited amount of movements that are well defined to unlock our phones. It simple describes a juggle routine which would be mapped by the user on the setup of the lock.

Refined Sketches

After all of our initial sketches, we decided to refine 3 of them. Those 3 were:

  • Tilt your phone game to unlock your phone (original idea from Hima)
  • Using AR (Augmented Reality) and a map to find something notable and unique to unlock your phone (original idea from Alan)
  • Using Body Proportions to unlock your phone (my idea)

Refined Sketch 1

Using the gyroscope and accelerometer, you can tilt your phone to drain the amount of water in 6 different drains. If you get the proportions correct, you can unlock your phone.

Refined Sketch 2

Using Alan's idea for AR, I thought giving a situation of a front-door and somewhere in the augmented space, a key is hidden (in this case, underneath a rug) to unlock your phone.

Refined Sketch 3

This is a combination (no pun intended) of my idea and Alan's idea for AR where you use AR to show a lock, and like a real lock, you have to input a correct number sequence while at the same time, the phone is analyzing your hands proportions to ensure that it is you.
