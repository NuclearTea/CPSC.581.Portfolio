Description

Tasked with creating something that helps with everyday tasks which must use some type of input sensor and some type of output actuation is quite open ended

At the time of ideation, there had been lots of snow in Calgary. As such, I had to shovel it which is quite tiring, encumbersome and can be uncomfortable depending on the weather. Our solution?

SNOOMBA

A "Roomba" that could analyze your driveway and clean the snow for you

Refinement

We essentially came down to 3 ideas, 1 from each of us.

First being my Snoomba idea

The Second being John's idea which involved strings that would make your bed for you

Lastly, Naheen's idea was an arrow which would work with google maps to show you the path to your desntiation and it would work like a compass

Storyboard

This depicts a typical driveway. It is snowing, heavily, with a thick layer of snow on the ground already.

The owners are too old to clean this. Attempting to do so could hurt them.

They let out their little friend, the Snoomba which will clean the snow for them

It has sensors which are like eyes, that detect whether there is snow infront of it and only shovling till the end of the driveway (as it has loaded an image of the driveway internally, similar to the roomba)

Reflection

Overall, this was my favourite group project. I liked the way we worked together and I really like the physicality of everything.

I really liked working with John and Naheen and I had a really fun time working on this project with them.

I was afraid that I was being a bit imposing (because we were talking about which of our 3 ideas to go with) however, we decided to go with mine (which I personally felt like was the best one) and I hope they are satisfied with the outcome.

Personally, I feel like it came out pretty well and we were able to incorperate other things to the demonstration (like our mock driveway with fake snow) which elevated the demonstration
