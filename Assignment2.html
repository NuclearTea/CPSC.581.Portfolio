Bop It

Description

When tasked to create a novel way of unlocking our phones, what better place to take inspiration from than childhood nostalgia. Presenting the Bop It unlock method. Using some shakes, twists, pulls and bops, you can unlock your phone in a secure manner, with the authentic sound of the original.

Security

In terms of its security, I would say that having a password that is at least like 6 motions long would be ideal as otherwise, it would be really easy to break in. Additionally, we do have sounds though, for highest security it would be better to turn the sound off so that others would not be able to hear it.

Accidental unlocks would pretty much be impossible with this approach as it requires a non-random complete set of movements to approve the password and fails otherwise.

The following are some initial sketches that show some of the inspiration for this idea.

Sketch 1

This is the most basic idea which pretty much everyone had which was: "track the movements of the phone and make that your password.". This idea is the base of 'Bop-It unlock method', which really constrains the movements into 4 distinct categories.

Sketch 4

This was to create an adapter which could enter the charging port and the adapter had a key slot for a physical key. The user would need to use the adapter and insert the correct key to unlock their phone.

Sketch 7

This image is quite important as it was chosen to be one of the refined sketches. It describes using the peripherals of our reach and body proportions to unlock our phones. I thought this was a good idea because it uses our unique combination of body proportions to unlock our phones, making it quite safe and easy.

Sketch 9

This plays with the idea of having a specific, limited amount of movements that are well defined to unlock our phones. It simple describes a juggle routine which would be mapped by the user on the setup of the lock.

Reflection

I wanted to add some more things because I think our project is a bit simple conceptually but I like the fact that we all used on childhood nostalgia to create this. Even though it may be a bit simplistic, it comes from a place thats rooted in our experiences and think that gives the idea more substance. Additionally, I would have liked to add more things to make it more thematic, but combining a lock screen with a Bop-It is not the easiest thing to do.

In terms of the development of the idea, it was something that Alan and I came up with while sharing our sketches. I think we all had some cool ideas in our refined sketches, but they also seemed difficult to implement well. Considering the amount of time it took us to calibrate this, I'm glad we went with the simple option.
